Municipal Workflow Systems

The Challenge

The Development Engineering department at the Town of Innisfil faced a high volume of document reviews and departmental approvals managed through a manual pipeline. This reliance on human-centric processing created data fragmentation and significant bottlenecks.

Objectives

  • Latency Reduction: Automate repetitive administrative tasks to decrease the lead time for engineering approvals.
  • Intelligent Routing: Automate the categorization and distribution of engineering documents using natural language processing (NLP).
  • Collaborative Peer Review: Build a dedicated interface for peer reviewers to access, annotate, and approve reports in a centralized ecosystem.

The Implementation

  • Data Flow Architecture: Developed a system that eliminates manual data entry, reducing the margin for human error and ensuring data integrity across the lifecycle of a permit.
  • Centralization: Engineered a platform that synchronizes document status and review data across all stakeholders instantly, ensuring a single, verifiable source of truth.
  • Automated Peer Review Pipeline: Developed a bespoke digital environment for reviewers to process reports and documents, eliminating the inefficiencies of manual email loops. Provisioned a dedicated interface for peer reviewers to directly access, annotate, and approve reports.
  • Document Intelligence: Integrated AI validation checkpoints to scan and verify project metadata before the human review phase begins.

AI Audio Authentication | Neural Signal Research

The Vision

As generative audio reaches a point of auditory parity with human speech, I identified a critical security gap: the inability of traditional protocols to distinguish between organic resonance and high-fidelity synthetic clones. I built this system to act as a definitive authentication layer, detecting the "digital fingerprints" that human ears cannot perceive.

The Engineering

  • Signal Decomposition: I engineered a custom preprocessing pipeline to extract deep acoustic features from raw audio, transforming sound into Mel-spectrograms and MFCCs for multidimensional analysis.
  • Neural Architecture: I designed and trained a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) specifically optimized for high-frequency pattern recognition within spectral maps.
  • Anomaly Fingerprinting: The system is built to identify phase inconsistencies and harmonic distortions—telltale signs of generative speech models that occur during the synthesis process.
  • Independent Research: I curated the training datasets and refined the model through iterative testing to achieve high-confidence binary classification ("Real" vs. "Synthetic").

Hivegram | AI Agent Social Layer & Solana Integration

The Vision

I architected Hivegram to explore the next evolution of social infrastructure: a platform where Autonomous AI Agents interact seamlessly with human users within a decentralized environment. By integrating directly with the Solana Blockchain, I built a system that treats social interactions as verifiable on-chain events, enabling a new paradigm of transparent, agent-led digital ecosystems.

The Engineering

  • AI Agent Framework: I engineered the logic for autonomous agents capable of independent interaction, content synthesis, and real-time social engagement within the platform's feed.
  • Solana Web3 Integration: I developed the bridge between the application layer and the Solana network, implementing high-speed, low-cost on-chain verification for user and agent actions.
  • Scalable Data Architecture: I designed a custom backend to manage the unique data flow requirements of AI-human hybrid environments, ensuring sub-second state updates and real-time synchronization.
  • Full-Stack Synthesis: I managed the end-to-end deployment—from the low-latency frontend transition logic to the complex integration of decentralized wallet protocols and agent-side API pipelines.
